# Grid Balancing with Variable Solar Generation

## Ramp-rate management
Grid operators typically limit plant ramp rates to 10 percent of nameplate capacity per minute to preserve frequency stability. When solar output drops sharply due to passing clouds, fast-start gas peakers or battery inverters must cover the shortfall within seconds. [Source: IEA Renewables Integration 2022]

## Reserve scheduling
Operators hold spinning reserves of at least 3 percent of forecast solar output during daytime hours to absorb forecast error. If the day-ahead forecast shows low confidence, reserves are scaled up to 5-7 percent. [Source: NREL Technical Report TP-6A20-77318]

## Forecast-informed dispatch
Economic dispatch windows of 5 to 15 minutes allow operators to re-balance generation as short-term solar forecasts refine. Intraday market participation reduces curtailment by up to 30 percent on variable-irradiance days. [Source: IRENA Power System Flexibility 2018]

## Frequency response
Solar inverters with grid-forming capability can provide synthetic inertia and fast frequency response, replacing functions traditionally handled by synchronous generators. Required droop setting is typically 4-5 percent. [Source: IEEE 1547-2018]

## Curtailment as last resort
Curtailment of solar is used when transmission is congested or reserves are exhausted. Well-managed systems keep curtailment below 5 percent of annual solar generation. [Source: CAISO 2023 Annual Report]
